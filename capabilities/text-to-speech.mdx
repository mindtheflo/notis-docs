# Text to Speech

Notis.ai enables you to convert text to natural-sounding speech through simple voice or text commands. This guide explains how to use the text-to-speech feature effectively.

## Getting Started with Text to Speech

<Steps>
  <Step title="Enable Text to Speech">
    If not already enabled, activate the text-to-speech feature:
    - "Enable text to speech" to Notis
  </Step>
  
  <Step title="Convert Your First Text">
    Try a simple text-to-speech command:
    - "Read this text aloud: [your text]"
    - "Convert this note to speech"
    - "Read my latest email to me"
  </Step>
  
  <Step title="Save or Share the Audio">
    When you receive the audio:
    - "Save this audio to my files"
    - "Share this recording via [platform]"
    - "Send this audio to [contact]"
  </Step>
</Steps>

## Advanced Features

<AccordionGroup>
  <Accordion title="Voice Customization">
    Customize the voice characteristics:
    
    - Voice types: "Use a deep male voice" or "Use a young female voice"
    - Accents: "Speak with a Scottish accent" or "Use an Australian accent"
    - Emotions: "Read this with excitement" or "Say this in a calm voice"
    - Character: "Read this like a news anchor" or "Speak like a storyteller"
    
    These options help you get exactly the voice style you want.
  </Accordion>
  
  <Accordion title="Audio Formatting">
    Control how the audio is formatted and delivered:
    
    - Speed: "Read this 20% faster" or "Slow down this reading"
    - Pauses: "Add longer pauses between paragraphs"
    - Emphasis: "Emphasize important words" or "Read with more expression"
    - Background: "Add soft background music" or "Include ambient sounds"
    
    These controls help create more engaging and effective audio.
  </Accordion>
  
  <Accordion title="Long-Form Content">
    Convert longer content to speech:
    
    - "Convert this entire article to an audio file"
    - "Read my entire research document"
    - "Create an audiobook from this text"
    - "Read all my notes from yesterday"
    
    Notis can handle converting lengthy content into well-paced audio.
  </Accordion>
  
  <Accordion title="Audio File Management">
    Manage your converted audio files:
    
    - "Save this as an MP3 file"
    - "Create a playlist of my converted notes"
    - "Organize my audio files by date"
    - "Delete audio files older than one week"
    
    These commands help you keep your audio content organized.
  </Accordion>
</AccordionGroup>

## Best Practices

<AccordionGroup>
  <Accordion title="Optimize Text for Speech">
    Format your text to sound better when spoken:
    - Use punctuation to create natural pauses
    - Spell out abbreviations and acronyms when needed
    - Include phonetic spellings for unusual names or terms
  </Accordion>
  
  <Accordion title="Choose the Right Voice">
    Different voices work better for different content:
    - Professional voices for business content
    - Expressive voices for creative content
    - Matching accents to the content's origin when appropriate
  </Accordion>
  
  <Accordion title="Test Before Sharing">
    Always listen to the audio before sharing it with others to ensure:
    - Correct pronunciation of technical terms or names
    - Appropriate pacing and emphasis
    - Overall clarity and quality
  </Accordion>
  
  <Accordion title="Consider Context">
    Think about how and where the audio will be used:
    - Shorter, clearer audio for mobile listening
    - More detailed audio for desktop use
    - Background noise considerations for different environments
  </Accordion>
</AccordionGroup>
