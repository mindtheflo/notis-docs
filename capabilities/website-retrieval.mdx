## Getting Started with Website Retrieval

<Steps>
  <Step title="Enable Website Retrieval">
    If not already enabled, activate the website retrieval feature:
    - "Enable website retrieval" to Notis
  </Step>
  
  <Step title="Retrieve Your First Website">
    Try a simple website retrieval command:
    - "Get information from https://example.com"
    - "Summarize the content on [website URL]"
  </Step>
  
  <Step title="Save or Analyze the Result">
    When you receive the extracted information:
    - "Save this information to my Research database"
    - "Extract the key points from this website"
    - "Compare this with my existing notes on the topic"
  </Step>
</Steps>

## Advanced Features

<AccordionGroup>
  <Accordion title="Multi-Page Analysis">
    Analyze content across multiple pages or sections of a website:
    
    - "Compare the pricing pages of [website1] and [website2]"
    - "Analyze all blog posts on [website] from the last month"
    - "Extract information from all subpages of [URL]"
    - "Gather content from these related URLs: [URL1], [URL2], [URL3]"
    
    This feature helps you gather comprehensive information from complex websites.
  </Accordion>
  
  <Accordion title="Content Filtering">
    Filter and focus on specific types of content:
    
    - "Extract only the tables from [URL]"
    - "Get images with captions from [website]"
    - "Find all quotes on [webpage]"
    - "Extract numerical data from [URL]"
    
    Content filtering helps you focus on the most relevant information.
  </Accordion>
  
  <Accordion title="Periodic Monitoring">
    Set up regular checks for website changes:
    
    - "Monitor [URL] for changes weekly"
    - "Alert me when [website] updates its pricing"
    - "Check [webpage] daily and notify me of any changes"
    - "Track new content on [blog URL]"
    
    This feature helps you stay updated on important websites without manual checking.
  </Accordion>
  
  <Accordion title="Content Transformation">
    Transform website content into different formats:
    
    - "Convert this article to bullet points"
    - "Create a table from the data on [URL]"
    - "Transform this webpage into a structured outline"
    - "Extract this content as markdown"
    
    Content transformation makes it easier to work with the retrieved information.
  </Accordion>
</AccordionGroup>

- Saved directly to your designated databases
- Organized with tags and categories
- Linked to related notes and projects

## Best Practices

<AccordionGroup>
  <Accordion title="Be Specific">
    Specify exactly what information you're looking for rather than retrieving entire websites. This saves time and provides more focused results.
  </Accordion>
  
  <Accordion title="Verify Information">
    Always check the source and credibility of websites you retrieve information from, especially for important research or decisions.
  </Accordion>
  
  <Accordion title="Organize Systematically">
    Develop a consistent system for organizing retrieved website content in Notion, such as using standard tags, categories, or dedicated databases.
  </Accordion>
  
  <Accordion title="Combine with Other Features">
    For best results, combine website retrieval with other Notis features like Perplexity Search for comprehensive research and analysis.
  </Accordion>
</AccordionGroup>
