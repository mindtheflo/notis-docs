# Listen to Content with Notis.ai

Notis.ai makes it easy to convert text content into audio, allowing you to listen to your notes, articles, emails, and other content on the go. This guide explains how to effectively use Notis for listening to content.

## Getting Started with Listening to Content

<Steps>
  <Step title="Choose Content to Listen To">
    Select what you want to convert to audio:
    - "Convert my latest note to audio"
    - "Read this article aloud: [URL]"
    - "Turn this email into speech"
  </Step>
  
  <Step title="Customize Audio Settings">
    Optionally adjust how the content will sound:
    - "Use a female voice"
    - "Read at a faster speed"
    - "Use a British accent"
  </Step>
  
  <Step title="Listen and Manage">
    Receive and manage your audio content:
    - Listen directly through your messaging app
    - Save audio for later listening
    - Share audio with others
  </Step>
</Steps>

## Advanced Features

<AccordionGroup>
  <Accordion title="Voice Customization">
    Customize the voice characteristics for your audio content:
    
    - Voice types: "Use a deep male voice" or "Use a young female voice"
    - Accents: "Read with a Scottish accent" or "Use an Australian accent"
    - Emotions: "Read this with excitement" or "Use a calm voice"
    - Character: "Read like a news anchor" or "Speak like a storyteller"
    
    These options help you get exactly the voice style you want for different types of content.
  </Accordion>
  
  <Accordion title="Playback Controls">
    Control how your audio content is delivered and played:
    
    - Speed: "Read this 20% faster" or "Slow down this reading"
    - Pauses: "Add longer pauses between paragraphs"
    - Emphasis: "Emphasize important points" or "Read with more expression"
    - Background: "Add soft background music" or "Include ambient sounds"
    
    These controls help create a more personalized listening experience.
  </Accordion>
  
  <Accordion title="Content Filtering">
    Focus on specific parts of longer content:
    
    - "Read only the introduction and conclusion"
    - "Skip the technical details when reading"
    - "Focus on sections about [topic]"
    - "Read just the key points"
    
    Content filtering helps you listen to only the most relevant information.
  </Accordion>
  
  <Accordion title="Audio Playlists">
    Create collections of audio content:
    
    - "Create a playlist of my recent notes"
    - "Add this article to my commute playlist"
    - "Make a sequence of all emails from today"
    - "Compile my research articles into one audio file"
    
    Playlists help you organize multiple pieces of audio content for continuous listening.
  </Accordion>
</AccordionGroup>

## Best Practices

<AccordionGroup>
  <Accordion title="Optimize Text for Listening">
    Before converting text to audio, consider:
    - Breaking long paragraphs into shorter sections
    - Adding punctuation for natural pauses
    - Spelling out abbreviations and acronyms
    - Including phonetic spellings for unusual terms
  </Accordion>
  
  <Accordion title="Choose the Right Content">
    Not all content works equally well in audio format:
    - Narrative and explanatory content works best
    - Highly visual or reference content may be less effective
    - Content with many tables or charts may need simplification
    - Consider creating audio summaries of complex visual content
  </Accordion>
  
  <Accordion title="Create Listening Routines">
    Develop habits around listening to content:
    - Morning news and updates during breakfast
    - Professional development during commutes
    - Meeting summaries while exercising
    - Learning materials before bed
  </Accordion>
  
  <Accordion title="Manage Your Audio Library">
    Organize your audio content effectively:
    - Delete audio files after listening to save space
    - Create themed playlists for different contexts
    - Tag audio content for easy searching
    - Set expiration dates for time-sensitive content
  </Accordion>
</AccordionGroup>
