## Getting Started with Copywriting

<Steps>
  <Step title="Define Your Content Needs">
    Specify what type of content you need:
    - "Write a social media post about [topic]"
    - "Create an email newsletter about [subject]"
    - "Draft a product description for [product]"
  </Step>
  
  <Step title="Provide Context and Details">
    Give Notis the necessary information:
    - Target audience and tone
    - Key points to include
    - Length requirements
    - Call to action
  </Step>
  
  <Step title="Review and Refine">
    Improve the generated content:
    - "Make this more conversational"
    - "Shorten this to 280 characters"
    - "Add more emphasis on [benefit]"
  </Step>
</Steps>

## Content Types

<Tabs>
  <Tab title="Social Media">
    **Platform-Specific Content**:
    - "Write a Twitter thread about [topic]"
    - "Create an Instagram caption for [image description]"
    - "Draft a LinkedIn post about [professional topic]"
    - "Generate a Facebook update announcing [news]"
    
    **Best Practices**:
    - Specify character limits for platforms like Twitter
    - Request hashtag suggestions when relevant
    - Ask for variations to test different approaches
    - Include a clear call to action
  </Tab>
  
  <Tab title="Email">
    **Email Types**:
    - "Write a welcome email for new subscribers"
    - "Create a follow-up email after [event]"

  </Tab>
  
  <Tab title="Website Copy">

  <Tab title="Marketing Materials">

## Advanced Features

<AccordionGroup>
  <Accordion title="Tone and Voice Customization">
    Customize the tone and voice of your copy:
    
    - Brand voice: "Write this in [brand]'s voice"
    - Emotional tone: "Make this sound more [excited/serious/urgent]"
    - Writing style: "Write this in the style of [author/publication]"

    These customizations help ensure your copy resonates with your specific audience.
  </Accordion>
  
  <Accordion title="SEO Optimization">

## Copywriting Best Practices

<AccordionGroup>
  <Accordion title="Know Your Audience">
    Before creating copy, clearly define:
    - Who will be reading this content
    - What they care about and value
    - Their level of familiarity with your topic
    - What action you want them to take
    
    The more specific you are about your audience, the more effective your copy will be.
  </Accordion>
  
  <Accordion title="Focus on Benefits, Not Features">
    When describing products or services:
    - Emphasize how they solve problems
    - Highlight the emotional benefits
    - Show the transformation they enable
    - Connect features to real-world advantages
    
    People buy benefits, not features.
  </Accordion>
  
  <Accordion title="Create a Strong Hook">
    Start your copy with something that grabs attention:
    - Ask a provocative question
    - Share a surprising statistic
    - Tell a relatable mini-story
    - Make a bold statement
    
    The first few seconds determine whether someone will continue reading.
  </Accordion>
  
  <Accordion title="Edit Ruthlessly">
    After creating your initial draft:
    - Remove unnecessary words
    - Replace jargon with clear language
    - Break up long sentences and paragraphs
    - Read aloud to check flow and naturalness
    
    Great writing is clear, concise, and compelling.
  </Accordion>
</AccordionGroup>

## Example Copywriting Workflows

<Steps>
  <Step title="Social Media Campaign">
    1. "Create a series of 5 Twitter posts about [new product]"
    2. "Make these posts more conversational and add relevant hashtags"
    3. "Create variations of the call to action for each post"
    4. "Save these to my Social Media database in Notion"
  </Step>
  
  <Step title="Product Description">
    1. "Write a product description for [product name]"
    2. "Emphasize these key benefits: [list benefits]"
    3. "Add technical specifications in a scannable format"
    4. "Create a shorter version for mobile display"
  </Step>
  
  <Step title="Email Newsletter">
    1. "Draft an email newsletter about [monthly updates]"
    2. "Include sections for: news, tips, upcoming events"
    3. "Generate 3 subject line options"
    4. "Make the content more personal and conversational"
  </Step>
  
  <Step title="Website About Page">
    1. "Write an About Us page for [company]"
    2. "Include our mission: [mission statement]"
    3. "Add sections for team, history, and values"
    4. "Make the tone professional but approachable"
  </Step>
</Steps>
